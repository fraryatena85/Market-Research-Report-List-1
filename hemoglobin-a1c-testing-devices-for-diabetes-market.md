<p><p>Hemoglobin A1c testing devices for diabetes utilize various methodologies to measure glycemic control over time. Boronate affinity chromatography separates glycated hemoglobin from non-glycated forms. Direct enzymatic assays provide rapid and accurate measurements through enzyme reactions. Immunoassays use antibodies to detect HbA1c levels. Ion-exchange HPLC separates components based on charge, while micro-optical detection methods leverage light to analyze samples. Capillary electrophoresis offers quick separation based on charge and size, ensuring precise HbA1c quantification for diabetes management.</p></p>
