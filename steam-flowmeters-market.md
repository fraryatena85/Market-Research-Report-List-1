<p><p>The steam flowmeters market is segmented into several types based on measurement principles. Orifice flowmeters utilize a differential pressure method to determine flow rates, while turbine flowmeters rely on the mechanical action of a rotating rotor. Ultrasonic flowmeters use sound waves to measure flow velocity, and vortex flowmeters capture the frequency of vortices shed by a bluff body. Other types may include electromagnetic and Coriolis flowmeters, each serving specific applications in industries like HVAC, manufacturing, and energy production.</p></p>
