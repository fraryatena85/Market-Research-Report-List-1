<p><ul><li>Deferasirox</li><li>Deferoxamine</li><li>Deferiprone</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Iron Overload Treatment Market includes three primary types of chelating agents: Deferasirox, Deferoxamine, and Deferiprone. Deferasirox is an oral medication commonly used for managing chronic iron overload, particularly in non-transfusion-dependent conditions. Deferoxamine is typically administered intravenously or via subcutaneous injection, focusing on severe overload cases, especially in patients with thalassemia. Deferiprone, also an oral chelator, is effective against iron overload, particularly in cases resistant to other treatments. Each agent has distinct administration methods, efficacy profiles, and patient preferences, influencing market dynamics.</p></p>
