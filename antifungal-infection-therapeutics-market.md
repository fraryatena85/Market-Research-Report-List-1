<p><p>The antifungal infection therapeutics market encompasses several drug classes. Polyenes, such as amphotericin B, disrupt fungal cell membranes. Echinocandins target cell wall synthesis, making them effective against Candida and Aspergillus species. Azoles, including fluconazole, inhibit sterol synthesis and are widely used for various infections. Allylamines like terbinafine focus on fungal cell membrane components. Other drug types may include newer agents and combination therapies, all contributing to the diverse strategies for managing antifungal infections effectively.</p></p>
