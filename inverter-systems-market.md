<p><p>Inverter systems are classified into three primary types: square wave, modified sine wave, and pure sine wave inverters. Square wave inverters produce a simple square waveform, suitable for basic applications like small motors but less efficient for sensitive electronics. Modified sine wave inverters generate a more refined output, compatible with various devices but may cause inefficiencies in some appliances. The market for these inverters reflects diverse needs, ranging from low-cost solutions to higher-quality options, catering to varying power requirements and user preferences.</p></p>
